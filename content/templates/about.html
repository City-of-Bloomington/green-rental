About

It's not rocket science, just smarter housing!

RentRocket collects information about rental housing over time to give you better insight into your next home.

Apartment listings come and apartment listings go. Similarly, sites that show active listings come and go.

RentRocket takes a different approach. RentRocket collects information about rental housing the rest of the time... things like utility costs and proximity to transit systems... things we hope will help you find a home that better meets your needs.

RentRocket provides the information renters and landlords need to make decisions that are both economically smart and strengthen the community.

RentRocket enables:

  1. Renters to see and compare utility costs, accessibility to alternative modes of transit and recycling options to make rental housing decisions that align with their values and save them money
  2. Landlords to promote and achieve a maximum return on investment for the energy saving improvements they have made to their properties.

RentRocket moves the search for rentals in a direction that is better for the future and for our communities.

All the information presented on RentRocket is shared by renters and landlords for renters and landlords. Join us in sharing information about your current properties or current rental.

Future

Collecting this information takes time, and we need your help. The easiest way is to share data about your current rental.

Want to get things moving in your city? Help organize data collection efforts. Have a data collection party! Here are some notes to get started.

If you are a property manager with access to information about multiple properties, we have a template you can use to send us your information in bulk.

We are actively seeking new partners to help take this project to the next level. Partner with us!

Are you a developer? Do you know a developer? Need a project for your local civic hack-a-thon? RentRocket is an open source project. Our code is available on GitHub.

Special Thanks

In 2013, 14 cities partnered together to obtain an an Innovation Fund grant from the Urban Sustainability Network and Global Philanthropy Partnership to create RentRocket.org. The City of Bloomington, Indiana has taken the lead on the project, and continues to move the project forward in partnership with these 10 other cities:

Albany (NY), Ann Arbor (MI), Berkeley (CA), Burlington (VT), Columbia (MO), Dearborn (MI), Evanston (IL), Iowa City (IA), Madison (WI), San Antonio (TX)

We are focusing on these four cities to illustrate the proof of concept:

  • Bloomington, Indiana
  • Columbia, Missouri
  • Ann Arbor, Michigan
  • Evanston, Illinois
